You are a document structure analyzer. Your task is to decide where to split a large markdown document into logical sections.

## Document Statistics
- Total characters: {total_chars}
- Estimated tokens: {estimated_tokens}
- Total lines: {total_lines}

## Header Structure (with line numbers)
The structure below contains the relevant headers from the ENTIRE document.
```
{header_tree}
```

## Your Task
Analyze the header structure and identify SPLIT POINTS (line numbers).
We need to clearly separate the Front Matter (Table of Contents, Preface/Foreword) from the Main Content, and then split the Main Content by MAJOR chapters.

CRITICAL RULES:
1. **FULL COVERAGE**: The input text is very long. You MUST scroll down and process the header tree util the VERY END (line {total_lines}).
2. **DO NOT STOP EARLY**: Do not stop at line 8000 or 10000. Continue until the last chapter (Index/References) at the end.
3. FRONT MATTER SEPARATION: You MUST distinguish between the **Table of Contents (TOC)** and the **Preface/Foreword**. They should be in separate chunks.
4. TOC COMPLETENESS: Identify where the Table of Contents (TOC) starts and ends. Note that TOC might list chapters with their starting line numbers or page numbers.
5. PREFACE COMPLETENESS: Identify where the Preface or Foreword starts and ends.
6. CHAPTER SPLITS: List the starting line number of EVERY major section (e.g., TOC, Preface, Chapter 1, Chapter 2). Each major section MUST start a new chunk.
7. STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY: Splits should prioritize the physical and logical structure of the book. 
   - A "Chapter" is a major split.
   - Meta-sections like "List of Theorems", "List of Figures", or "List of Tables" should be GROUPED TOGETHER into a single "Front Matter" chunk if they are short and adjacent, rather than creating a tiny chunk for each.
   - Do not split in the middle of a chapter unless absolutely necessary for very large files.
8. MINIMUM CHUNK REASONABILITY: Avoid creating "micro-chunks" (less than 10 lines) for repetitive or navigational elements (like lists of theorems) unless they are clearly separated by major content.
9. SPECIAL SECTIONS & ATOMIC BLOCKS: Identify highly cohesive sections that MUST NOT be split to preserve logical continuity. This includes:
   - EXERCISES, PRACTICE, QUIZ, PROBLEM SETS
   - ANSWERS, SOLUTIONS, HINTS (these often appear at the end of the book/chapter and must remain intact, as splitting them breaks the Q&A mapping)
   - APPENDIX, REFERENCES, INDEX, GLOSSARY
   These should be marked as `atomic_ranges` (to be protected from further splitting, regardless of their length).
10. Output ONLY the extracted line numbers in the JSON format.

## Output Format
Return ONLY a JSON object:
```json
{
  "toc_range": [50, 611],
  "preface_range": [612, 817],
  "chapter_splits": [50, 612, 818, 1438, ..., 14000, 15000],
  "atomic_ranges": {
    "Exercises": [[2500, 2800]],
    "Appendix": [[15000, 15500]],
    "References": [[15501, 16000]]
  }
}
```
- `toc_range`: The [start, end] line numbers for the Table of Contents.
- `preface_range`: The [start, end] line numbers for the Preface or Foreword.
- `chapter_splits`: A flat array of integer line numbers where each major section (including TOC and Preface) starts.
- `atomic_ranges`: (Optional) A dictionary mapping section types to their line ranges that should NOT be split further.

Return ONLY the JSON string. No prose.
